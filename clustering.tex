%% Abstract section.
\abstract{%
This paper presents a summary about various techniques to detect and identify densely connected
nodes in a graph, so called clusters. In the first part, we introduce the concept of clusters for
static graphs alongside their main properties. For dynamic graphs with time varying edge connections,
these cluster may be subject to change with every time step. Therefore additional characteristics
have to be introduced.

The second part describes two methods to detect, identify and track cluster in a dynamic graph.
A common solution for this problem is the clustering of a static graph at each time step, and the
identification of the same clusters over multiple time steps. A method is presented to track these
clusters, which is independent of the underlying static graph clustering algorithm.
Furthermore, we describe an extension of the k-clique percolation algorithm to dynamic graphs.

Finally, the clique percolation algorithm is applied to two different real world networks, which
yields interesting result about group dynamics, with regards to the correlation of various group
properties.
} % end of abstract

\firstsection{Motivation}
\maketitle
  
  The concept of graphs was always an important part of computer 
  science. Their not only able to represent a wide variety 
  of data structures, but also allow to model any kind of relationship
  between multiple entities or processes in a network. Therefore, graphs
  are nowadays used in a wide variety of applications, from group
  dynamics in biological networks[?], over control methods in multi agent systems
  to communication patterns in social networks.
 
  Traditionally, new theories and algorithms were tested against so called
  random graphs, in which the probability for a connection between each 
  node of the network is constant. Due to their probabilistic nature, 
  these random graphs have an binomial degree distribution. But research 
  has shown, that real world networks often have an anomalous degree distribution
  which follows approximately a power law. This means that a few nodes
  are highly connected, while most others have only a few connections
  within the graph. Furthermore, these networks often consist of locally
  densely connected subgraphs, often referred to as clusters or communities.
  
  With the rise of the world wide web and the emerging increase of electronic
  communication, a lot of interest shifted toward the detection and identification
  of these communities within networks.  
  
 This paper will focus on the more densely connected, local parts of these real
  world networks, which are called clusters or communities. 
  
  With the rise of social networks, 
  Not only is it crucial for social media marketing

  vor allem wegen social networks und daraus social marketing sind dynamic
  graphs mehr ins licht gerueckt. dynamische graphen sind graphen mit varierenten
  kanten. hierbei spielt vor allem die kommunikation, und die daraus enstehenden
  untergruppen des netzwerks (cluster) eine grosse rolle. man mochte herausfinden
  wie sich solche gruppen finden lassen, wie sie das netzwerk beeinflussen,
  und sich im verlauf der zeit entwickeln.
